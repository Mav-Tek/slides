Culture Changes

Introduction
James addressed the technical aspect of how we're addressing accountability.
Now I'll address the cultural aspect.
As developers, we want to fix everything, but we can't. We require everyone to contribute.
Can we modify people's behavior with software?

Voluntary vs. Enterprise

  • Contributing because you want to
  • Contributing because you are paid to
Goal of Wikipedia is to offer the sum of all human knowledge.
Goal of enterprise KM is to maintain sum of all knowledge specific to org.
In both cases, I think you want as many people as you can get from your group to contribute.
Otherwise, you’re unlikely to record all knowledge, and your information is more likely to be biased toward participants’ viewpoints.

Goals

Without continuous contributions and qualified reviews from everyone in your community, a wiki is just another dumpster of outdated and unreliable information.
In an enterprise environment, you might just say "your job requires you to use the wiki"
But what does that mean? 20% viewing? 15% contributing 2x/week?
Wiki goals must align with org goals and be specific

Refocus our goals

Instead of focusing on adding content, our focus is now on the quality of our content.


Even Wikipedia is criticized for focusing on quantity over quality (Source)

Up until we discovered the problems mentioned earlier in this presentation, focus was on adding content
Now our focus is on ensuring content is reviewed properly.

Motivation

Extrinsic vs. Intrinsic

How do we get users to do desired behaviors to address goals?
Need to identify what motivates users.
Dollar/day will eventually not work. Need intrinsic motivation.

Intrinsic motivation

  • Competence
  • Autonomy
  • Relatedness
Competence: Challenging accomplishment
Autonomy: User in control of environment, need options
Relatedness: Something beyond the person, greater good, social interaction

User Personality Types

Based on an interview with Amy Jo Kim
  • Competitors - Act on other users (challenge, showoff, compare)
  • Self-Expressers - Act on content (create, build, design, customize)
  • Explorers - Interact with content (view, collect, review, curate)
  • Collaborators - Interacting with users (share, comment, help)
1 approach won't work for everyone
Self-expression: Careful that templates don't preclude creativity
Explorers: Exactly what Watch Suggestions does
Collaborators: Thanks, future dev on social interaction/sharing

Online Communities

  • In a zero-sum game, we are opponents: One wins, others lose
  • In a non-zero-sum game, we are partners: We all win or we all lose
    • Wiki environment is not about 1 person winning, but about a community winning
    Competition should be about the product, not the person
    Fair playing field: Offer options
